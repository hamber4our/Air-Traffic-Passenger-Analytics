# Air-Traffic-Passenger-Analytics

## Project Overview

This project analyzes Air Traffic Passenger Statistics data to uncover trends in passenger traffic, airline performance, regional distribution, terminal utilization, and airport operations. The dashboard was built in Microsoft Excel using Pivot Tables, Pivot Charts, KPIs, Slicers, and interactive visualizations.

The analysis covers passenger traffic data from 1999 to 2025, representing over **1.1 billion passenger movements**.

---

## Objectives

* Analyze long-term passenger traffic trends.
* Identify the highest-performing airlines.
* Evaluate passenger distribution across geographic regions.
* Assess terminal and boarding area utilization.
* Compare domestic and international passenger traffic.
* Create an interactive dashboard for operational insights.

---

## Data Cleaning

The following data preparation steps were completed:

* Checked all columns for missing values.
* Retained 316 missing Operating Airline IATA Code records because airline names were available.
* Retained 2 records with Passenger Count = 0 as valid observations.
* Removed unnecessary metadata columns.
* Created helper columns:

  * Year
  * Month
  * Year-Month
  * Quarter
* Verified data quality before analysis.

## Key Insights

### Passenger Traffic Trends

* Passenger traffic grew steadily from 1999 to 2019.
* A significant decline occurred in 2020 due to the COVID-19 pandemic.
* Traffic recovered strongly from 2021 onward and approached pre-pandemic levels by 2025.

### Airline Performance

* United Airlines was the dominant carrier with over 260 million passengers.
* Historical United Airlines records prior to July 2013 contributed nearly 200 million additional passengers.
* American Airlines, SkyWest Airlines, and Delta Air Lines ranked among the top-performing carriers.

### Geographic Analysis

* US passenger traffic accounted for approximately 76% of total traffic.
* Asia (10%) and Europe (7%) were the largest international regions.

### Terminal Analysis

* Terminal 3 handled approximately 454 million passengers and accounted for roughly 41% of total traffic.
* The International Terminal processed over 282 million passengers.
* Terminal 1 processed more than 261 million passengers.

### Boarding Area Analysis

* Boarding Area F processed approximately 335 million passengers, making it the busiest boarding area.
* Boarding Areas B and G followed with approximately 149 million and 145 million passengers respectively.

### Price Category Analysis

* Non-low-fare carriers accounted for approximately 87% of passenger traffic.
* Low-fare carriers represented approximately 13% of total passenger volume.
